Grammar — Will vs Going To

Planned decisions vs spontaneous decisions

10 min ▼

GOING TO — planned, already decided

Use going to for plans and intentions you have already decided before speaking.

Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb
I'm going to visit my sister next weekend. (already planned)
She's going to start a new job in September. (decided before)
Look at those clouds — it's going to rain! (evidence now)
🇹🇷going to = önceden kararlaştırılmış plan. "Bu hafta sonu kardeşimi ziyaret edeceğim" (planladım, kararlaştırdım)

WILL — spontaneous decision / prediction / offer

Use will for decisions made at the moment of speaking, predictions, and offers.

Subject + will + base verb (contraction: 'll)
A: "We're out of milk." B: "I'll get some!" (decided NOW)
I think it will rain tomorrow. (prediction — no evidence)
I'll help you with that! (offer)
✗ I will go to the cinema tonight. (if already planned → going to)
🇹🇷will = o an alınan karar / teklif / tahmin. Önceden planlanmamış, anında karar verilmiş
Going To (plan)
"I'm going to see a film tonight" — booked tickets already!
Will (spontaneous)
"The film looks great — I'll watch it!" — decided just now.
Simple guide: Already planned before speaking? → Going To. Decided just now? → Will.
