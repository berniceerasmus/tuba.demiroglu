Grammar — Stative Verbs & Time Clues

Never continuous + the words that guide your choice

20 min ▼

Stative verbs — NEVER use continuous

Stative (non-action) verbs describe a state or condition, not an action. Because states have no real beginning or end, these verbs are only used in simple tenses — never continuous.

✗ I am knowing the answer.
✓ I know the answer.
✗ She is loving Istanbul.
✓ She loves Istanbul.
🇹🇷 Turkish uses seviyorum for love — present continuous. But English uses Simple: I love Istanbul. NOT I am loving Istanbul.

4 categories of stative verbs

Stative verbs fall into four groups. Learn one group at a time.

💼 Possession & size
belong, own, have*, possess, weigh, measure
💭 Opinion & thought
know, believe, agree, think*, imagine, understand
❤️ Likes, dislikes & feelings
like, love, hate, want, need, wish, prefer, dislike
👁 Perception & senses
sound, hear, seem, see*, appear, look*, taste*, smell*, feel*

* These verbs can sometimes be dynamic — see the dual-use table below.

Dual-use verbs — stative AND dynamic

Some verbs have two meanings. When they describe a state → simple. When they describe an action → continuous is possible.

VerbStative → simpleDynamic → continuous OK
havepossess → I have two cats.experience → They are having a party.
bedescribe → James is a doctor.behave → He is being very kind.
thinkbelieve → I think it's a good idea.reflect → She is thinking about her trip.
lookappear → Tom looks like his sister.focus eyes → Peter is looking out the window.
taste / smell / feelsense → This soup tastes great.test / act → She is tasting the soup.

Time expressions — your clues

🟢 Present Simple
  • always / usually / often
  • sometimes / never
  • every day / week / morning
  • on Mondays / at weekends
🔵 Present Continuous
  • now / right now
  • at the moment / currently
  • today / this week / this year
  • these days
