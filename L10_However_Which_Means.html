Grammar — However & Which Means

Contrast and consequence at B1 level

18 min ▼

However — showing contrast between sentences

However is like "but" — it shows contrast. But "however" is more formal and comes at the start of a NEW sentence (or after a semicolon). It always has a comma after it.

Turkish: Ancak / Bununla birlikte / Fakat

Sentence 1. However, sentence 2.
✓ I love Istanbul. However, Ankara is very special to me too.
✓ The exam was difficult. However, I passed!
✗ However it was difficult, I passed. (wrong position — use "although" here)
ConnectorPositionExample
althoughStart of a sentence, before a clauseAlthough it was late, I stayed.
howeverStart of a NEW sentence + commaIt was late. However, I stayed.
butMiddle of a sentenceIt was late but I stayed.

Which means — explaining consequences

Which means adds an explanation or result. It comes after a comma and refers back to the whole previous idea. Great for making your speech sound more B1!

Turkish: Bu da demek oluyor ki / yani

Main idea, which means [explanation/result].
✓ Neva is turning one in April, which means she'll soon start walking!
✓ I've finished Phase 1, which means I'm now a B1 learner.
✓ The train was cancelled, which means I had to drive instead.
Tuba's goal: In every lesson from now on, try to use at least ONE "however" and ONE "which means" when speaking. These two alone will make your English sound much more B1!
