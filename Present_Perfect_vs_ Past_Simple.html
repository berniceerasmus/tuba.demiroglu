Grammar — Present Perfect vs Past Simple

The most important tense distinction at B1+

10 min ▼

THE KEY PRINCIPLE

Both tenses refer to the past, but they frame it differently. Past simple treats an event as finished and done. Present perfect connects the past to the present in some way.

Specific completed time → PAST SIMPLE
I saw her yesterday. / He left in 2021. / She arrived at six.
Unspecified time / link to present → PRESENT PERFECT
I've seen her recently. / He's left. / She's arrived.
✗ I have seen her yesterday. — "yesterday" forces past simple.

TIME EXPRESSIONS — THE SORTING RULE

These always force past simple:

yesterday · last week/month/year · in 2019 · at 6pm · on Monday · when I was young · ago

These typically go with present perfect:

ever · never · already · yet · just · today · this week · this year · recently · since · for (duration)
🇹🇷Zaman belirtilmişse (dün, geçen hafta, 2020'de) → Past Simple. Zaman belirsizse ya da &351;imdiyle bağlantysa → Present Perfect.
✗ Wrong
I have seen her yesterday at the market.
✓ Correct
I saw her yesterday at the market.
✗ Wrong
Did you ever visit Rome? / Have you visited Rome last year?
✓ Correct
Have you ever visited Rome? / Did you visit Rome last year?

Exercise 4 Free writing.

Two Perspectives on the Same Story

Write 7-8 sentences about something that happened in the past that still has some relevance or consequence today.
