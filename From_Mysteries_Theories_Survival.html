What are Parts of Speech?

Every word in English has a job. We call these jobs parts of speech. The four most important ones are:

NounA person, place, or thing."The creature appeared at night."
VerbAn action or a state."They investigate the strange symbols."
AdjectiveDescribes a noun."The town is mysterious and dangerous."
AdverbDescribes a verb or adjective."She looked around carefully."

Knowing the part of speech helps you use a word correctly and build longer, more natural sentences in English.

Grammar Focus 1 — Relative Clauses (NEW)
⏱ 10 mins
What is a Relative Clause?

A relative clause gives extra information about a person, thing, or place — without starting a new sentence. We use it to make our sentences longer, richer, and more natural.

whoUse for people."Boyd is the man who protects everyone."
whichUse for things."The talisman is an object which keeps you safe."
whereUse for places."The town is a place where nobody can escape."

The relative clause comes directly after the noun it describes. The verb tense inside the clause does not change.

who → for people     which → for things     where → for places
Examples from FROM
Boyd is the man. He tries to protect everyone.
Boyd is the man who tries to protect everyone.
The creatures come out at night. They are dangerous.
The creatures are monsters which come out at night.
The town traps people. Nobody can escape there.
The town is a place where nobody can escape.
Victor knows many secrets. He has lived there for years.
Victor is a character who knows many secrets.
Practice — Combine the sentences using who, which, or where
1.Victor knows many secrets. / Victor has lived there for years.
→ Victor is a man who has lived there for years and knows many secrets.
2.The town is dangerous. / Everyone is trapped there.
→ The town is a place where everyone is trapped.
3.The tree appears often. / The tree may have special powers.
→ The tree is something which may have special powers.
4.The talismans protect people. / They hang on every door.
→ The talismans are objects which hang on every door and protect the people inside.

What is Reported Speech?

Reported speech (also called indirect speech) is how we tell someone what another person said — without using their exact words. We do not use quotation marks. Instead, the verb tense moves back one step into the past.

DirectBoyd said: "We need answers."
ReportedBoyd said that they needed answers.
DirectVictor said: "I have seen this before."
ReportedVictor said that he had seen it before.

Key tense changes:   is/are → was/were  ·  need → needed  ·  will → would  ·  can → could  ·  have seen → had seen

is/are → was/were    need → needed    have seen → had seen    will → would    can → could
FROM Characters — Direct → Reported
"We need answers." — Boyd
Boyd said that they needed answers.
"I have seen this before." — Victor
Victor said that he had seen it before.
"We must find a way out." — Tabitha
Tabitha said that they had to find a way out.
Practice — Change to reported speech
1.Jim: "This place is dangerous."
→ Jim said that this place was dangerous.
2.Donna: "We need to stay together."
→ Donna said that they needed to stay together.
3.Victor: "I know something important."
→ Victor said that he knew something important.
4.Boyd: "We cannot give up."
→ Boyd said that they could not give up.

Speaking — How to express opinions in English

In English, strong opinions are built with opinion starters + a reason. The best answers at B1+ level use complete sentences, connecting words (because, however, although, which means that), and specific examples from the show.

Weak answer"I think the town is a simulation."✗ Too short — no reason given
Strong answer"My theory is that the town is a simulation, which would explain why nobody can find the exit."✓ Opinion + reason + relative clause

Try to speak in full sentences and use at least one opinion starter from the list below each time.

?What do you think the town really is?
?Why do you think people can't leave?
?Which character do you trust the most — and why?
?Which mystery is the most interesting to you?
?What would you do if you arrived in the town?
?What do you think the creatures really want?
?Is Victor a good person or a bad person?
?How do you think Season 2 will end?
Opinion starters — encourage Tuba to use these
I believe that... My theory is that... In my opinion... One possibility is that... It seems likely that... I strongly suspect that... From the evidence we have seen... I agree / I disagree because...
