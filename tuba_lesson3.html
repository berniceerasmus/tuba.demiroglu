Grammar — Present Perfect Formation

have/has + past participle — the key building block

10 min ▼

HOW TO FORM PRESENT PERFECT

Present Perfect = have / has + past participle

I / You / We / They → have + past participle
He / She / It → has + past participle
I have visited Paris. · She has eaten sushi. · We have finished.
✗ I have visit · She has ate — WRONG! Must use past participle.
🇹🇷Present Perfect = have/has + fiilin 3. hali (past participle). Regular fiiller için -ed ekle. Irregular fiillerde: eat→eaten, go→gone, see→seen

CONTRACTIONS (kısa form)

In speaking and informal writing, we almost always use contractions:

I have → I've · You have → You've · We have → We've · They have → They've
He has → He's · She has → She's · It has → It's
I've been to London. · She's eaten sushi. · We've finished!

KEY TIME EXPRESSIONS — EVER / NEVER / ALREADY / YET / JUST

ever = in questions: Have you ever been to Paris?
never = not at any time: I have never eaten snails.
already = before expected: I've already finished!
yet = in negatives/questions: I haven't called yet. Have you eaten yet?
just = very recently: She's just arrived!
🇹🇷ever/never = hiç · already = zaten/çoktan · yet = henüz (olumsuz/soru) · just = az önce
