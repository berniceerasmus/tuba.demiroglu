Grammar — Although: Showing Contrast

How to build complex, B1-level sentences

15 min ▼

What does "although" mean?

Although connects two contrasting ideas — it shows that something surprising or unexpected happens despite another fact. It means the same as even though.

Turkish equivalent: ...masına / ...mesine rağmen — "rağmen"

Although + [surprising/negative fact], [result/main idea].
✓ Although it was raining, we went for a walk.
✓ Although she was tired, she finished the work.
Position: "Although" can come at the start OR in the middle of a sentence.
✓ Although I was late, I didn't miss anything.
✓ I didn't miss anything, although I was late.
❌ Tuba's pattern (fragmented)
It was cold. But we had fun.
✓ B1 complex sentence
Although it was cold, we had a great time.
❌ Common mistake
Although it was expensive, but I bought it.
✓ Correct — no "but" after although
Although it was expensive, I bought it.
🇹🇷 Common error for Turkish speakers: never use "but" after "although" — in Turkish you can add "ama" but in English you can't!

Tuba's life — although in context
