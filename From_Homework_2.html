Relative Clauses — who / which / where

A relative clause gives extra information about a person, thing, or place without starting a new sentence. It comes directly after the noun it describes.

who → for people  ·  which → for things  ·  where → for places

who → people     which → things     where → places
Boyd is the man. He protects everyone.
Boyd is the man who protects everyone.
The talisman keeps people safe. It hangs on the door.
The talisman is an object which keeps people safe.
The town traps people. Nobody can escape there.
The town is a place where nobody can escape.
Reported Speech — tense changes

Reported speech tells someone what another person said — without using their exact words. The verb tense moves back one step into the past.

Direct speechReported speech
is / arewas / were
need / needsneeded
willwould
cancould
have / has seenhad seen
musthad to
know / knowsknew
"We need answers." — Boyd
Boyd said that they needed answers.
"I have seen this before." — Victor
Victor said that he had seen it before.
"We must find a way out." — Tabitha
Tabitha said that they had to find a way out.
