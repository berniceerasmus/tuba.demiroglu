Present Perfect Formation

have/has + past participle · ever/never/already/yet/just▼
Formation: I/You/We/They + have · He/She/It + has · + past participle. Contractions: I've, she's, they've etc. Negatives: haven't / hasn't.

L04

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Specific time → PS · No specific time → PP▼
Key rule: Specific time word (yesterday, last week, in 2020, at 6pm)? → Past Simple. No specific time (ever, never, already, just, yet, today, this week)? → Present Perfect.

Will vs Going To

Already planned → going to · Decided now / prediction → will▼
Key rule: Going to = plan decided BEFORE speaking. Will = decision made AT the moment / prediction / offer.
Exercise 1 — Fill in

4

Exercise 3 — Error Correction
1
I'm going to meet my sister for dinner. (spontaneous offer → going to — already booked restaurant)
2
I'll help you! ('m going to → 'll — spontaneous decision)

Exercise 4 — Writing: Check going to sentences have a pre-decided plan context, will sentences show spontaneous decision/prediction/offer.

Connectors

because · so · but · although · however▼
Quick guide: WHY? → because · RESULT? → so · CONTRAST (same sentence)? → but · SURPRISING CONTRAST? → although · CONTRAST (new sentence)? → However. Never use because + so together.
